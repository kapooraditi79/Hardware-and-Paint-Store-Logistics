Emulsion paint comes in various types based on finish, purpose, and formulation, each suited for specific applications. 

By Finish
    Matt Emulsion: Offers a flat, non-reflective surface that hides wall imperfections. Ideal for ceilings and living areas where a soft, elegant look is desired. Modern versions are often durable and washable. 
    
    Satin/Soft Sheen Emulsion: Provides a subtle gloss with enhanced durability and easier cleaning. Suitable for high-traffic areas like hallways and kitchens. 
    
    Silk Emulsion: Delivers a smooth, silky sheen that brightens rooms and enhances light reflection. More resistant to wear than matt finishes. 
    
    Eggshell: A low-sheen finish between matt and satin, offering better washability and a refined appearance. 
    
    Textured Emulsion: Adds dimension and artistic effect (e.g., stucco, metallic, vintage finishes). Used for decorative or feature walls. 

By Purpose
    Interior Emulsion Paint: Designed for walls and ceilings indoors. Focuses on smoothness, stain resistance, and low odour. Often water-based with acrylic or vinyl resins. 
    
    Exterior Emulsion Paint: Formulated to withstand UV rays, rain, dust, and fungal growth. Contains additives for weather resistance and crack-bridging flexibility. 
    
    Washable Emulsion Paint: Engineered for high-traffic or child-friendly spaces (kitchens, bathrooms). Features anti-stain, anti-fade, and high cleanability. 
    
    Low-VOC/Odour-Less Emulsions: Ideal for nurseries and bedrooms. Minimizes harmful emissions for better indoor air quality. 
    
    Specialty Finishes: Include anti-bacterial, anti-algal, and metallic variants for specific aesthetic or functional needs. 

By Composition
    Acrylic Emulsion: Water-based, durable, fade-resistant, and eco-friendly. Commonly used for interiors. 
    
    Vinyl (Acrylic) Emulsion: Similar to acrylic but often more affordable. Offers good coverage and quick drying. 
    
    Alkyd Emulsion: Typically oil-based, more durable and glossy, but less common in modern water-based systems.

By Budget & Quality
    Luxury: Premium finishes with advanced features (e.g., crack resistance, high sheen). 
    
    Premium: Balanced durability and cost.
    
    Economy: Budget-friendly with reliable coverage and decent performance.
    
    
    
For best results, match the emulsion type to the surface and environment—use interior emulsion for walls and ceilings, exterior emulsion for outdoor surfaces, and washable or textured variants for functional or decorative needs.
